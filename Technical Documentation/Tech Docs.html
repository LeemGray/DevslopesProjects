Introduction

Racing is a sport that has recently boomed massively in popularity as a result of Drive to Survive on Netflix.

To put it simply, racing is a bunch of cars, going from formula cars to hot hatchbacks, going around a predetermined track for a specific amount of time or laps, and who ever finishes with the fastest time, or before the other cars...wins!

Disciplines of Motorsport

There are many different kinds of racing, with the most popular types falling under these categories

  • Formula Racing - Heavily regulated open wheeled cars that are typically built around aerodynamics
  • Rally Racing - Bravery personified...typically hot hatchbacks with a driver and co-driving telling him where the turns are
  • Stock-Car Racing - Big loud cars going in circles! Basically a chessmatch at 200mph
  • Endurance Racing - A real test of drivers and engineering. Races can last from 6 to 24 hours with driver changing and different classes of cars sharing a track

Why we Race

Because we like to go fast and we like to win! There's something therapeutic about striving to complete the perfect lap.

From the engineering perspective, building a car that is faster, stronger, and more reliable than other manufacturers is a special pride point

From a business perspective...marketing

Racing Line

This is the most important part of unlocking speed. Horsepower and grip are good but you can't get good times if you can't maximize the track

A common misconception in racing is that the line in a corner is the shortest distance from A to B but that's not completely true! The real fastest line is what allows to to carry the most amount of speed for the exit

The rule of thumb is "slow in-fast out"

Using Tires

Managing tires is a very close second to unlocking lap time

They are not omnipotent so tip-toeing on the limit of your tires at all times is key.

Accelerating, braking, and turning are your tires' main jobs. They cannot give you full grip doing one thing if they're doing another, treat it like a budget
